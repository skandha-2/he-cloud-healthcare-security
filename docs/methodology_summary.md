# Research Methodology Summary

## Research Problem

Healthcare organizations increasingly rely on cloud-based databases for storing and processing sensitive patient data. Traditional encryption protects data at rest and in transit, but many workflows still expose data during processing. Homomorphic encryption and related privacy-preserving approaches may reduce data-in-use exposure, but adoption is constrained by performance overhead, implementation complexity, key management maturity, and vendor support.

## Research Questions

1. What is the relationship between advanced encryption use and reported security outcomes?
2. What is the perceived performance impact of homomorphic encryption and advanced encryption methods?
3. How does advanced encryption adoption relate to compliance readiness?
4. Can encryption adoption predict security outcomes when controlling for organization size and cloud provider?
5. What technical or organizational factors influence successful implementation?

## Methods Demonstrated in This Repository

- Literature synthesis
- Compliance mapping
- Architecture modeling
- Descriptive statistics
- Correlation analysis
- Group comparison
- Regression-ready dataset structure
